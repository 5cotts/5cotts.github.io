about

I started coding when I was young by reverse engineering websites and creating my own static HTML webpages (much like this one). Despite this, I found myself studying economics and business in college. After convincing myself I wanted to become a CPA, and then convincing myself I wanted to become a PhD economist, I found myself coding again and haven't stopped since. Unlike when I was younger, I now see the power technology has and how it can impact the world, for better or worse. My goal is to develop technologies that benefit society.

I also have experience in cloud administration, database administration, Linux systems administration, and DevOps. I have completed coursework in statistics & mathematics which allows me to have a basic level of competence in some data science concepts (I can explain how linear regression works and what neural networks are) but am always eager to learn more about this topic and find ways to apply it in my work.
